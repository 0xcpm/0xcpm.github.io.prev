what is a copyleft license?

a copyleft license is a type of license that allows individuals to freely use, modify, and distribute a work, provided that any derivative works are also distributed under the same license. this ensures that the original freedoms granted by the creator are preserved for all future users and developers.

how does it work?

  • the creator licenses their work under a copyleft license, such as the creative commons attribution-sharealike license (cc by-sa).
  • anyone can use, modify, or distribute the work, but they must comply with the terms of the license.
  • derived works must also be distributed under the same copyleft license.

popular copyleft licenses

examples of widely used copyleft licenses include:

  • creative commons attribution-sharealike (cc by-sa)

benefits of copyleft

using a copyleft license provides several advantages:

  • encourages collaboration: promotes a community of shared innovation.
  • protects openness: ensures that derivative works remain open-source or free to access.
  • supports creators: respects the original creator's intent to keep their work freely available.

how to apply a copyleft license

to use a copyleft license for your work:

  1. choose a suitable license, such as the cc by-sa.
  2. include the license text or a reference to it in your work (e.g., in the readme file or a license header).
  3. clearly state the license terms to ensure others understand their rights and obligations.
